# English Minimal Pairs — Index for Pronunciation Practice

Source: EnglishClub.com pronunciation series (scraped 2026-06-13).
Use this alongside `cmudict.dict` (CMU Pronouncing Dictionary) for IPA lookups.

A minimal pair = two words differing by exactly one phoneme. They isolate the
phoneme contrast for perception + production drills.

## Notes for the tutor

- **Stars (★)** mark the highest-priority contrasts for Spanish L1 learners (your
  Louisville/La Casita base). Drill these first.
- Pair selection should follow the **learner's L1 inventory** — don't drill /l/-/r/
  with Spanish speakers (not confusable for them), do drill /b/-/v/ and /θ/-/s/.
- For voice-tutor use: (1) play one word, ask which they heard; (2) ask learner to
  produce both; (3) embed in minimal-pair sentences ("It's a **bad** bed" vs "It's a
  **bed** bed") to force semantic disambiguation.
